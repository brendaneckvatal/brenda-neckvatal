# Ambiguity as Leverage

## Definition

A dynamic where unclear expectations, undefined standards, or open-ended communication are used to avoid accountability or reinterpret decisions.

## Why It Matters

Ambiguity enables toxic behavior by making enforcement feel subjective and negotiable.

## Practical Meaning

Leaders must reduce gray areas through explicit standards, confirmed understanding, and consistent follow-through.
